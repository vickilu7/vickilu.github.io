Side Project

Let me first start by saying that Medium is one of my absolute three favorite services created (the other two being Spotify and Typeform). With its minimalistic and sophisticated UI as well as intuitive UX, the Medium app is nearly perfect in my eyes. However, as a user, I’ve found myself wishing it did have a more extensive bookmarking system.

Description of Project

Problem
As of now, you can only either save articles you liked to your “Bookmarks” or to your “Claps”– offering only two lists to differentiate your articles.

Solution
Create a feature that allows users to categorize their favorite articles into different categories like Pinterest boards or Youtube playlists. In the case of Medium, I decided to categorize the bookmarks into “shelves”.

Process and Work

Inspiration
These “shelves” act as the topics in which users can sort their articles, much like how you’d place similar genre books near each other on a bookshelf. The way the bookmarking looks on the home page is inspired by Airbnb. I felt that the clean and straightforward look of the Save to List modal window fit in quite well with Medium’s overall brand.

High Fidelity mockups with Adobe XD
I recreated the Medium homepage to be more conscious of how my new feature would fit with the existing look and feel. The following pictures show the three groups of pages I made changes to for the new bookmarking function.

Something I did forget to add, but would be great to have, is an option to share your shelf. Medium is an inherently social platform that allows anyone to write and share ideas, so I think it’s always a good idea to keeping thinking about the ways we can connect people with people.
