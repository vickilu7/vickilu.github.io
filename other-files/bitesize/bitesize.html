I worked as a UI/UX designer with a team of quality improvement experts, clinicians, programmers and researchers at Duke Cancer Institute to create BiteSize QI.

Background

Quality improvement (QI) is an integral component to delivering high-quality healthcare.  However, QI is challenging, and with mounting day-to-day duties, healthcare workers struggle to find the time to learn proper skills to successfully affect change in their workplace.

BiteSizeQI was created to foster micro-innovation within large organizations.  Micro-innovation promotes small, positive changes to be made to an existing process by stakeholders that interact with the process.  We believe that the best ideas don’t always come from the top and, when given the right tools, all staff can drive meaningful change.

How it Works

BiteSize QI is a web-based application that helps users identify a problem or deficiency in their workplace, and then walks the user through the fundamentals of quality improvement to help fix the problem. 

Upon logging in, users will work their way through a series of short, Lean Six Sigma-inspired videos that build their quality improvement skills.

After completing a video, the system then engages the user to apply the skills they have learned by completing simple quality improvement activities.

As a user completes each activity, the system records the progress made on the dashboard and builds a guide for implementing a solution to the problem using the responses from the activities.

We call this guide a Project Blueprint. BiteSizeQI gives all members of a team a voice to highlight problems in the workplace and propose fixes for those problems in a structured way consistent with validated quality improvement principles.
